How has crime in Malaysia
changed over time?

Crime in Malaysia has changed significantly over the past decade. While overall crime rates have generally declined, different categories of crime have followed very different patterns. Property crimes such as vehicle theft and burglary remain among the most common offences, while violent crimes such as murder and assault occur less frequently but continue to shape public perceptions of safety. These visualisations explore how crime trends have evolved over time and which types of offences continue to affect Malaysians the most.

How does crime differ
across states?

Crime is not distributed evenly across Malaysia. Urban and densely populated states often record higher crime rates, while smaller states may experience very different patterns of criminal activity. Socioeconomic conditions, population size, and regional differences all contribute to these variations. This section explores how crime rates differ across Malaysian states and highlights which areas experience the highest levels of criminal activity over time.

How does crime differ
across states?

Patterns of imprisonment also vary across Malaysia. Some states maintain consistently larger prison populations, while others show lower incarceration levels despite changes in crime rates. Gender differences are also significant, with male prisoners making up the vast majority of the prison population nationwide.
